Alarm System

📱
Easiest option: Text Andy or Michelle before you enter. They can disarm the alarm remotely from their phone via the alarm.com app — no panel needed.
Disarming the Alarm Yourself (ADT alarm.com Panel)

If Andy or Michelle aren't available, here's how to disarm the ADT touchscreen panel yourself. You'll have about 30–60 seconds after entering before the alarm sounds.

  • 1
    Walk directly to the alarm panel — it will be beeping with an entry countdown.
  • 2
    The touchscreen will display the system status. Tap "Disarm" on the screen.
  • 3
    Enter the 4-digit alarm code Andy or Michelle texted you using the keypad on the screen.
  • 4
    The panel will announce the system is disarmed, the beeping will stop, and the screen will show a green status.
💡
Made a mistake entering the code? Just try again — the panel allows multiple attempts. If you enter the wrong code, it will beep twice and let you re-enter.
Setting the Alarm When You Leave
  • 1
    Make sure all windows and doors are closed (see the Check-Out list below).
  • 2
    Text Andy or Michelle — they'll arm the alarm remotely from their phone. Easy!
  • 3
    Head out and enjoy the drive home. 🌊
⚠️
If the alarm goes off: Enter your 4-digit alarm code immediately and tap Disarm. ADT will call Andy or Michelle's cell directly. Text them too so they know what's happening.

  • The hot tub is always on and self-regulating — just remove the cover and get in

Weber Genesis Grill

The grill is a Weber Genesis connected to natural gas — no propane tanks to worry about. Here's how to use it safely.

⚠️
Safety first: Always open the lid before lighting. Never light with the lid closed — gas can build up and cause a flare-up. Keep the grill at least 10 feet from the house, fences, and any flammable materials.
Before You Light — Check the Gas Valve
  • 1
    Locate the natural gas shutoff valve on the gas line running to the grill (see label on or near the grill).
  • 2
    The valve handle should be parallel to the pipe — that means gas is ON. If it's perpendicular (across the pipe), turn it to open it.
  • 3
    Make sure all burner knobs on the grill are in the OFF position before proceeding.
Lighting the Grill
  • 1
    Open the lid fully. This is the most important step — never skip it.
  • 2
    Push in and turn the leftmost burner knob to the Start / High position.
  • 3
    Press the igniter button on the front panel. You'll hear a click and the burner will light.
  • 4
    Light the remaining burners left to right, one at a time, the same way.
  • 5
    Let the grill preheat for 10–15 minutes with the lid closed before cooking.
💡
Igniter not working? Turn all burners OFF and wait 5 minutes for gas to clear. Then use a long lighter or match through the match-light hole near the burners. Never lean over the grill while lighting.
Shutting the Grill Down
  • 1
    Turn all burner knobs to OFF.
  • 2
    Turn the gas shutoff valve so the handle is perpendicular to the pipe — this cuts the gas supply.
  • 3
    Leave the lid open for a few minutes to let the grill cool, then close it.
  • 4
    Once cool, place the grill cover back on.
🧼
Please give the grates a quick brush after cooking while the grill is still warm. Grill brushes and tools are stored in the kitchen island drawer closest to the porch. If you run into any issues, text Andy or Michelle.

Kitchen Gas Stove

The kitchen has a gas stove — just two things to keep in mind every time you cook.

💨
Always turn on the range hood fan before cooking. The stove runs on gas and the fan removes fumes and prevents grease buildup. Even for a quick boil — fan on first.
Every Time You Cook
  • Turn on the range hood fan before lighting any burner
  • Light burners using the knob — turn to ignite, then adjust to your flame

Local Cape Cod Guide

Our favorite spots near the house — enjoy Cape Cod like a local!

🏊 Middle Pond & The Private Beach

Middle Pond is one of the three interconnected Indian Ponds — over 100 acres of clear, clean freshwater right outside your door. Perfect for swimming, kayaking, and fishing.

🏖️ Private Beach — Indian Lakes Association
As guests of the Webb home, you have access to the private association beach near 390 Mystic Drive. This is a residents-only beach — a lovely, quiet spot on the pond, great for kids. No parking fee, no crowds.

📍 Get Directions: 390 Mystic Drive, Marstons Mills

🚣 Pond Access for Kayaks & Canoes
The public launch is at the end of Flume Avenue off Mystic Drive (from Route 149). Note the 10 HP motor limit on all Indian Ponds.

Fish species: largemouth bass, yellow perch, chain pickerel, and pumpkinseed. Bring a rod!

📍 Pond Launch: Flume Ave, Marstons Mills
